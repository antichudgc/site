IFAK - Individual First Aid Kit

What is it? Why would I need one?

The IFAK is not a first aid kit kit in the traditional sense, being used to treat minor abrasions / burns, but can be thought of as a first aid kit for gunshot or other traumatic injury. The contents are designed to treat life threatening hemorrhage and stabilize the injury until the patient can be transported and treated at a higher level of care. If you're regularly at protests or actions where armed people will be present, you should consider carrying an IFAK.

Contents

Minimum - If you can only buy one piece of medical equipment, this is it.

  • Minimum of 1 Tourniquet (CAT is the only type I would recommend) - Most important piece in the kit. Used to stop life threatening blood loss from limbs. Make sure this is staged correctly and ready for use.
  • Sharpie - Write the time of application on the Tourniquet if used.

Basic - After the tourniquet

  • Nitrile gloves
  • Bandages / Pressure dressings - Used to apply pressure and help control bleeding.
  • Trauma Shears - Used to cut away clothing, gear, etc when required to easily access and treat wounds.
  • Chest seals - These should come in a pair. Used to seal chest wounds and prevent air from entering the chest cavity.

Intermediate - Mostly because hemostatic dressing is expensive.

  • Hemostatic dressing - Quikclot Combat Gauze, Celox Z-Fold, etc. You want the dressing not granules.

Advanced - These items require specialized training to use. You won't be able to self aid with these items. Very optional.

  • 1x Nasopharyngeal Airway (NPA) - Secure an airway which otherwise may be obstructed.
  • 1x Decompression Needle 14ga or larger, minimum 3.25" length - Diagnosing and treating tension pneumothorax isn't for amateurs, unless you're regularly around highly trained medics, it's probably best to skip this one.
